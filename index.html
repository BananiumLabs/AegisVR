About Aegis

Aegis is a first-person simulator that utilizes both virtual reality and motion tracking to provide immersive lessons on self-defense. From breaking out of one's grip to disarming an assailant carrying a weapon, each lesson is designed to educate users in safely handling situations where other individuals pose a direct threat.

Begin a Lesson

The primary focus of self-defense is not to win the fight, but to hurt your opponent enough for you to escape. If you're faced with a possible dangerous situation, your first instinct should be de escalation and you should attempt to remove yourself from the area, while remaining alert to threats. If you are faced with an assault and have no escape, make as much noise as you can to attract attention and use principles taught by experienced self-defense instructors.

Intro to Targeting

 There are 5 key pressure points on the body that are sensitive to damage, which you can use to your advantage against your assailant. These points are the eyes, nose, throat, groin, and knees.

Targeting Practice

 If caught in a hand-to-hand fight, do your best to dodge your opponent's attacks and try to grab onto their arm/leg if you can in order to deny them use of that limb. Then, when they are exposed, go for an easily accessible pressure point. You can also use their energy against them, punishing their advance with an attack of your own.

Gun Defense

 Guns are rarely used at close range, but one is put against your head, you have a chance of escaping it. A fact you must realize is that once you touch the gun at all, it will go off. So, the best strategy is to first get yourself out of the gun's line of fire. The best direction would be up , but left and right can work too (go in the direction opposite your dominant hand). Move while grabbing the gun with both hands.

 After the gun has fired, or when it's already been raised, push into your opponent's body and take yank the gun away from their hand, putting as much pressure on their arm(s) as you can. After you yank the gun way, put distance between yourself and your assailant while chambering a new round into the barrel to be ready to fire if necessary. Increase distance and run if there is an escape route available.

 For the purpose of this video, we will be using a plastic water bottle in place of a gun.

Knife Defense

  Knives are very dangerous in close quarters situations, even more so than guns. Part of that danger is their ability to be concealed and used very quickly. In fact, most victims don't even see the knife during the attack and only realize they've been stabbed after the assault ended. Due to this potential danger it is very important to watch the hands of any suspicious people around you, in case they may be carrying a blade.

  If you manage to see a knife, do your best to dodge the knife and grab the arm holding the knife. After that, push into your opponent's body and either take their arm over your shoulder to deny them its use and get them to drop the knife, or hit their elbow in order to get them to release the knife due to the instant surge of pressure. After the knife is dropped, throw or kick it away to buy you time for when you run in the opposite direction.

 For the purpose of this video, we will be using a plastic water bottle in place of a knife.
